Early Life (1809-1827)

  • Born on January 19, 1809, in Boston, Massachusetts.
  • Orphaned at a young age; raised by John and Frances Allan in Richmond, Virginia.
  • Attended the University of Virginia but left due to financial issues and conflicts with his foster father.

Military and Early Writing Career (1827-1836)

  • Briefly joined the U.S. Army and attended West Point but was discharged.
  • Published his first collection, Tamerlane and Other Poems (1827).
  • Began focusing on literary work, publishing short stories and poetry.

Professional Writing Career (1836-1845)

  • Married his cousin, Virginia Clemm, in 1836.
  • Worked as an editor and critic for various magazines, gaining a reputation for his literary acumen.
  • Published The Narrative of Arthur Gordon Pym of Nantucket (1838), his only novel.

Later Years and Success (1845-1849)

  • Gained fame with the publication of The Raven (1845).
  • Struggled with poverty and personal tragedy, including the death of his wife in 1847.
  • Died under mysterious circumstances on October 7, 1849, in Baltimore, Maryland.
